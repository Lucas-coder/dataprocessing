Mortality rates from neurological diseases in the European Union in 2015

A neurological disease can be defined by a disorder of the nervous system. Neurological diseases can have fatal consequences. Moreover, World Health Organization (WHO) data shows an increased global burden of mortality from neurological diseases from the early 1989-1991 to 2012-2014 [1]. Additionally, this increased burden is most visible in the USA. As a matter of fact, WHO data shows that the mortality rate from neurological diseases is higher in western countries than the rest of the world.

These WHO findings make it valuable to zoom in to a group of western countries, namely the European Union (EU). In the visualization a map with the EU countries is depicted (including some non-EU countries). The colors represent the mortality rate of the countries. Additionally, you can switch to a map that depicts the population of the countries. When a country is selected, the graph on the right shows the mortality rates of that country of Parkinson's Disease, Alzheimer's Disease and the other neurological diseases.

The main observations from this visualization are: 1) Alzheimer's Disease is greatest contributer to mortality rate; 2) the mortality rates appear higher in the Western European countries than in the Eastern European countries; 3) Finland appears to have a much higher mortality rate from neurological diseases than the other countries. While the first two observations are not explained yet, a paper in Brain Research has some interesting explanations for the high mortality in Finland [2].
